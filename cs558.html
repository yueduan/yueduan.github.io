Prerequisite
CS 450 or CS 455 or CS 458 The course assumes that the students have:
  • Basic knowledge about Network, Operating System and/or Crypto.
  • Familiarity with at least one programming language, such as C, C++, Java, or Python.
  • No prior security related research experience required.
Course Overview
This course will teach various modern topics in network and computer security. It will provide a thorough grounding in cyber-security for students who are interested in conducting research on security and networking, and for students who are more broadly interested in real-world security issues and techniques. Students will undertake a semester-long research project. The goal of this course is to introduce basic concepts as well as state-of-the-art techniques in this area, and let students gain hands-on experience by applying some of these techniques to solve real-world security problems.

Lecture topics will include, but not limited to:
  • Unwanted traffic, such as spam
  • Malware and defense mechanisms
  • Network/System configuration and defense, such as firewall, access control, intrusion detection systems
  • Cyber-physical system security, such as IoT, smart manufacturing
  • Other hot topics, such as deep-learning applications in security, blockchain security
Course Outcomes
  • Explore a range of problems modern network and computer security
  • Understand basic issues, concepts, threats, principles, and mechanisms in cyber-security
  • To understand how to engage in networking and security research, and to investigate novel ideas in cybersecurity through a semester-long research project
Sample project topics
  • A study on EOS blockchain smart contracts
  • Binary differential analysis based on decompilation
  • Statistical target scheduling for directed fuzzing
  • Program authorship detection
  • Binary alias analysis based on deep learning
  • Program deobfuscation
  • more to come!
Textbooks
There is no required textbook. Most readings will be from research papers in recent security conferences.

Course format and gradings
  • Paper presentation: 25%
  • Paper review: 10%
  • Discussion participation: 15%
  • Project 50% (proposal 5%, mid-term report 15%, final presentation 15%, final report 15%)

All late submissions (but still within one day after the deadline) will automatically lose half points. Submissions one day after the deadline will NOT be accepted (unless you get permission from the instructor).
There will be bonus points for (1) EXCELLENT research projects and (2) good in-class participation (attendance and discussion).
